Selected Honors

I received the NDSEG Fellowship which began the fall of 2019, as well as recognition in the form of Honorable Mention for the NSF GRFP award. I have received several travel awards, including the Ripple Society for Neuroscience Travel award and the Neural Interfaces Conference Diversity Travel award. I have additionally earned the Society for Pelvic Research Best Presentation and was selected to give a talk as part of a showcase of early stage researchers at the International Society for Autonomic Neuroscience.

Science

I study neuroscience and rehabilitation of the lower urinary tract. In my thesis, I aim to use epidural spinal cord stimulation to recruit bladder nerves and control both continence and voiding, build a computational model of the sacral spinal cord to understand the neural recruitment achieved with this stimulation, and produce effective bladder control in awake behaving animals. More information about these and other projects I have been involved in is located here.
